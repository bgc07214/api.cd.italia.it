Independent Members of Parliament will sit in a meeting with Awami League President and Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina at Ganabhaban this Sunday evening to take directions on what role they will take in the 12th National Parliament starting from January 30. Independent MPs who won a record number of 62 seats will meet at Ganobhaban at the invitation of the Prime Minister. In today’s meeting, Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina can give necessary directions regarding the role of individual parliamentarians and reserved seats.
\nTalking to several independent parliamentarians of Awami League, it is known that they will perform their duties in parliament as per instructions given by party president Sheikh Hasina regarding the role they will play in the national parliament for the next five years. Because, except for a few, all independent members of parliament are directly involved in Awami League politics and hold big positions. Therefore, the newly elected independent members of parliament are looking at the party leader.<\/p>\n

Awami League President and Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina will be happy to see them in the role, they will act accordingly. They are unwilling to take any risk of ending their political career by displeasing the party. Rather, independent MPs want to try to ensure their party’s nomination in the next election by proving their efficiency in the parliament.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
