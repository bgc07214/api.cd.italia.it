According to the report, the Ministry of Hajj and Umrah of Saudi Arabia has taken this initiative so that marriage can be conducted in the holy Mecca and Medina.<\/p>\n

Experts say that this is a great opportunity for various institutions to come up with exceptional ideas for organizing weddings there, while preserving the sanctity of these two mosques.<\/p>\n

A Saudi marriage official named Masoud Al Jabri said that there is religious consent in teaching marriage in mosques. The Holy Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) once taught the marriage of a colleague in the mosque. Many of the local people in Madinah now perform marriage ceremonies in the Masjid al-Nabawi. <\/p>\n

He said, Madina residents do this for various reasons. Many people in Medina traditionally invite all their relatives to the wedding. Most of the time the bride’s family cannot accommodate everyone in the house. As a result, marriage is taught by coming to the Prophet’s Mosque or the Kaaba.
